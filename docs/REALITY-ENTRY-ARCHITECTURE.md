## The governing principle

> **The stakeholder does not organize the architecture. The stakeholder defines the
> legitimate entry realities and bounds authority throughout the interaction.**

Reality is the invariant. **Reality organizes this catalog.** A stakeholder is not the
primary axis — it is *metadata* that does exactly two things: it makes a given reality
**legitimate** to bring into the organization, and it **bounds the authority** of
everything that follows (what may be known, decided, committed, and delivered). The same
reality may be brought legitimately by several stakeholders. So the catalog is ordered by
**Reality Entry**, and "Legitimate Stakeholders" is a column, not a section.

## How to read it (the columns are the chain)

Each reality moves through the locked seven nouns. The columns *are* that chain:

- **Reality Entry** — the true situation, in the person's own words. The invariant.
- **Legitimate Stakeholders** — who may legitimately bring this reality; metadata that
  bounds authority. (Entry *sources* — AI, Google, a physician's mention — are how it
  arrives, not who initiates; noted where relevant.)
- **Initial Capture** *(Perceive → Record)* — the *only* facts taken at first contact.
  The canon rule is universal: capture "only information necessary to move the relationship
  forward"; everything else is "progressively discovered later." Never an intake form.
- **Initial Understanding** *(Understand)* — what Alara now knows is going on; the
  regenerable synthesis of the captured reality. Owns no new state.
- **Initial Decision** *(Judge)* — what *should* happen next. Normative, accountable, the
  locus of human sovereignty.
- **Initial Commitment** *(Orchestrate → Act)* — what Alara binds itself to. Across the
  canon this is one promise — **close the loop** (confirm receipt immediately, then keep
  every party informed at each meaningful step) — plus a named Care Guide.
- **Participant Obligation** *(Communicate)* — what this participant **may know, must do,
  or should receive**, bounded by authority. The consent gate lives here.
- **Enabling Capability** *(Verify / Learn back the loop)* — the AlaraOS capability that
  makes the commitment honest. **No row may promise what no capability can deliver — the
  interface cannot lie.**

### Note on the DOL Resource Center (a non-stakeholder of record)

A Resource-Center-informed claimant **self-initiates** and enters as a patient-initiated
reality (rows 1–2 / 7). The Center "cannot recommend Alara" and "does not submit
referrals"; it is never a referring stakeholder of record and is owed **absolute respect
for its neutrality** and freedom from any sales pressure ("non-negotiable"). It therefore
appears as an entry *source*, not a row.

## The rendering rule for numeric commitments

> **Numeric operational commitments are rendered according to participant obligation.**

A published number is a **Commitment** in the architecture, not a marketing claim — so it
may only appear if AlaraOS can **continuously Verify** it. The number itself is not secret;
it is simply rendered to the participant for whom it is relevant. Same capability,
different interaction, same truth:

- **Referrer / professional surfaces** (physician, hospital, case manager, attorney, AR):
  concrete SLAs — "Discharge referrals responded to within one hour," "SOC acknowledgment
  within 24 hours." These reduce friction and build referral confidence.
- **Patient / family surfaces:** outcome-oriented commitments — "We respond quickly,"
  "You'll know exactly what happens next," "You'll know who owns your situation." Patients'
  reality is uncertainty; their obligation is clarity and ownership, not internal metrics.

## Cross-cutting invariants (apply to every row)

1. **Capture is minimal and progressive.** Take only what moves the relationship forward;
   discover the rest later. First contact is never an intake form.
2. **The first commitment is loop closure.** Confirm receipt immediately; keep every party
   informed at each meaningful step; never let anyone "wonder what happened."
3. **Authority bounds everything downstream.** Each participant may know, must do, and
   should receive only what their identity permits. Consent gates every outbound delivery.
4. **The interface cannot lie.** Every promise traces to a named Enabling Capability; every
   published number is a continuously-verified Commitment.
