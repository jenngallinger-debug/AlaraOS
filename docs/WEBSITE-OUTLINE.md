# The Alara Website — Outline by user, hook-first

> Status: DRAFT for owner sign-off, 2026-06-30. Reworked: the website's job is to **pull in
> people who are already searching for us, hook them immediately, prove why we're better, and
> route each known user to their path to convert.** It is the front door and the router, not the
> in-care experience. The Eight Experience Moments appear here as the **promise of how care will
> feel** (proof/desire), not as the site's structure. Derived from `docs/REALITY-ENTRY-ARCHITECTURE.md`
> (the user types), the frozen Eight Moments, and `docs/VISION.md`.

## 1. What the website is for

- **The visitor already knows roughly what they need.** Nobody random finds us; they are
  searching for an agency like us. So the site does not need to educate from zero — it needs to
  **hook, prove, and route fast.**
- **Five beats, in order:**
  1. **Hook** — the hero, and only the hero: who Alara is, what we do, why we're better, in a
     one-liner about the moat that makes them think *"this system is insane — I want these people."*
     **No decisions are asked here.** The hero does not route.
  2. **Lock it in** — transition straight into **AlaraOS, revealed like Apple reveals its chip:** a
     little pre-education on why Alara is unlike anyone else. This is what closes them emotionally
     ("ok, we've got them"). Light, not White-Card-deep.
  3. **Route to their lane** — *now* let them find their lane. Push them down **one** lane; don't
     make them weigh a pile of decisions.
  4. **In the lane: educate or convert** — if they need education, give the full thing but **dumbed
     down** (all the tea leaves and breadcrumbs), so they feel *"there's a lot here, and I need
     someone to do it all for me"* — then the **best clinical care** and the **founder's
     experience** to solidify. If they already know what they want, straight to convert.
  5. **Convert** — per stakeholder: qualify / have your physician refer / etc., never forgetting
     the family. At their pace, never pushed.
- **The differentiator is the moat:** Alara is always *a step ahead* — it knows before, keeps
  everyone in concert, and makes sure each person already has what they need. That is the hook and
  the "why better."
- **Two audiences, two front doors:** people who need care (for themselves/family), and
  professionals sending us a patient. Both are hooked in seconds, each in their own language.

## 2. The hero (the hook — no path decision)

The hero's only job is to make them want us. It carries:
- **Who we are + what we do:** specialized home health for White Card, federal-worker, and veteran
  patients in Las Vegas.
- **Why we're better, in one line:** Alara runs on AlaraOS and stays a step ahead of your care —
  the line that lands as *"this system is insane, I want these people."*

That's it. **No path chooser in the hero**, no "which of these is you" yet. We hook first; routing
comes after the AlaraOS reveal.

## 3. Patient paths (they need care for themselves or family)

There are only a few. Each is a known reality from the Catalog. For each: **who · what they're
searching · the hook · their path through the site · the value · convert · how it will feel.**

### Path A — "I have a White Card and need home care"  *(the bread-and-butter)*
- **Searching:** an agency that takes their White Card and actually knows it.
- **Hook:** "Your White Card covers more than anyone told you — and we deliver all of it, at no
  cost to you."
- **Journey:** Hero → choose this → **White Card pillar** (what it covers · the benefits most
  families miss · what Alara does / does not do) → **proof** (DON review, only MSW agency, the
  Catch) → **Begin** (start care) — fast, because they're eligible and decided. Navigator offered
  for "what applies to *me*."
- **Value given:** the clearest White Card explanation anywhere + the missed benefits (instant
  "I didn't know that").
- **Convert:** Begin.
- **How it feels (promise):** the weight lifted; never start over; they're always a step ahead.

### Path B — "I need home care, but I don't have a White Card (or I'm not sure)"
- **Searching:** home care, and quietly wondering if they're owed something.
- **Hook:** "You may be owed benefits you were never told about. We'll help you find out — and
  get you care either way."
- **Journey:** Hero → choose this → **Navigator** (triage: DOE/Test Site worker? veteran? federal
  worker? other coverage?) → routes to the **EEOICPA filing path** (no-card), the right program
  pillar, or general home care → **Begin**.
- **Value:** eligibility clarity and a real path, in minutes, no account.
- **Convert:** Navigator → Begin (or the filing path).
- **How it feels:** someone finally made this make sense; we take it from here.

### Path C — "I'm a veteran and need care at home"
- **Searching:** home care without the VA wait, without Medicare.
- **Hook:** "Home care without the VA wait — here's how it works."
- **Journey:** Hero → choose this → **VA Community Care pillar** (no wait, what's covered, how it's
  authorized) → **proof** → **Begin**. Goals-first, no pressure (per the veteran journey).
- **Value:** the VA Community Care answer, plainly.
- **Convert:** Begin.
- **How it feels:** heard, unhurried, taken care of.

### Path D — "I'm a federal or postal worker with an OWCP claim"
- **Searching:** does workers' comp cover home care, and who handles the paperwork.
- **Hook:** "Workers' comp covers home care — and we handle the authorization so you don't."
- **Journey:** Hero → choose this → **OWCP / FECA pillar** (yes it covers; the authorization path
  others get wrong; what's covered) → **proof** → **Begin**.
- **Value:** the authorization answer + the relief of not doing the paperwork.
- **Convert:** Begin.
- **How it feels:** the weight lifted; the system handled.

*(Family caring for a parent/spouse cross-cuts all four — they enter on the patient's behalf and
take the matching path; the copy speaks to the caregiver as well as the patient.)*

## 4. Professional paths (they have a patient; they need the best agency)

They landed because they're looking for an agency to trust with a patient. Hook them that **yes,
this is the place**, **why it's the best**, **why sending to us is good for the patient *and* for
them**, and **exactly how to refer and how it will feel.**

### Path E — Physician
- **Searching:** an agency they can trust without it creating work for their office.
- **Hook:** "Refer with certainty. We handle the care, the benefits, and the authorizations — and
  keep you informed without you ever chasing us." Why best: DON-led, complex-patient and
  federal-benefit specialists.
- **Journey:** Hero → professionals door → **Physicians page** (the services · why best for complex
  & White Card patients · the closed loop + concrete SLAs · refer in under two minutes, in your
  workflow) → **Refer**.
- **Why it's good for them:** less office work, reputation protected, proactive updates.
- **Convert:** Refer (fax / EMR / secure message / form).
- **How it feels:** "My patient is in good hands. I never have to chase Alara."

### Path F — Case manager / discharge planner
- **Searching:** a fast, reliable, often EEOICPA-specialized agency that makes a clean handoff.
- **Hook:** "Discharge referrals answered within one hour. We make the handoff clean — and make
  you look good."
- **Journey:** Hero → professionals door → **Referral page (case-manager view)** (one-hour
  response · the closed loop back to your unit · EEOICPA quick reference · how to refer) → **Refer**.
- **Why it's good for them:** speed, reliability, loop closure, made to look good.
- **Convert:** Refer.
- **How it feels:** "I'll know exactly what happened after the patient leaves my unit."

### Path G — Resource Center worker  *(neutral — never a sales target)*
- **Searching:** genuinely useful, neutral education for claimants; they cannot recommend a provider.
- **Hook:** *not a pitch* — "Educational resources your claimants can use, with no strings and full
  respect for your neutrality."
- **Journey:** Hero (or direct) → **Education/Library** + a neutral explainer → if a claimant
  chooses Alara on their own, they enter Path A/B.
- **Convert:** none in the traditional sense; provide materials; the claimant self-initiates.
- **How it feels:** respected, never used; no aggressive outreach (non-negotiable).

### Path H — Attorney / Authorized Representative / community partner
- **Searching:** an agency that protects their reputation and never makes work for them.
- **Hook:** "Send your clients to an agency that protects your reputation and closes every loop."
- **Journey:** Hero → professionals door → **Referral page (AR/attorney view)** (closed loop ·
  claim-boundary awareness · how to refer) → **Refer**.
- **How it feels:** "I don't have to think about it — I send them to Alara and it's handled."

## 5. How the homepage presents this (the order matters)

1. **Hero — the hook.** Who we are, what we do, why we're better, in one line. No decisions. (§2)
2. **The AlaraOS reveal — lock them in.** Reveal AlaraOS like Apple reveals its chip: the moat
   (knows before · everyone in concert · each person already informed), as light pre-education on
   why we're unlike anyone else. This is the "fuck yeah" beat that closes them. → links deeper to
   *The AlaraOS difference*.
3. **Now find your lane.** Only here does the **path chooser** appear: the patient paths (A–D) and a
   distinct **"Refer a patient"** door (E–H). Push them into one lane; minimal decisions.
4. **In the lane** (on the pillar / path page, or a homepage preview): full-but-simplified education
   (the tea leaves and breadcrumbs) → the best clinical care → the founder's experience to solidify.
5. **Convert** — per stakeholder: Begin / qualify (patients), Refer (professionals), and the family
   never forgotten. Those unsure of their lane go through the **Navigator**.

So the spine is: **hook → reveal AlaraOS → choose lane → educate (or go straight to convert) →
convert.** The hero hooks; AlaraOS locks; the lane carries them home.
