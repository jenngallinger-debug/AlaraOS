## The doctrine

**Reality is shared. Experience is contextual.**

There is one reality. It is the invariant. Everyone touched by a situation is living
the same reality. What differs is the experience each one should have of it,
appropriate to their role.

## The single chain (one world, not two axes)

This is one world. Each layer enables the next. These are not two parallel axes.

```
REALITY   (single source of truth)
   │
   ▼
REALITY OPERATING MODEL   (how the organization understands reality)
   Reality Graph · Reality Understanding · Judgment · Capabilities · Operating Cycle
   │
   │   enables accurate experiences
   ▼
STAKEHOLDER EXPERIENCE OF REALITY   (what each participant should experience, given that reality)
   │
   ▼
INTERACTIONS   (website · voice · portal · SMS · Care Guide · internal UI)
```

- **Reality is the organizing principle of the organization.**
- **The stakeholder's experience of shared reality is the organizing principle of the interfaces.**

## Same reality, different experience

One reality (a patient is becoming weaker, gait unstable, a cardiology appointment
missed, family out of state) produces role-appropriate experiences:

- **Daughter:** reassurance. "Someone has this."
- **Physician:** clinical awareness. "I have exactly what I need."
- **Nurse:** prioritized work. "I know what needs my attention."
- **Executive:** operational visibility.
- **Biller:** "This visit has documentation."

They are not experiencing different realities. They are experiencing the same reality
in the way appropriate to their role.

## The bridge

- The operating model says what is **possible**.
- The stakeholder experience of reality says what is **desirable**.
- The interaction is where they meet.

An interface is a render of the desired stakeholder experience, **enabled and
constrained** by the operating model beneath it.

## The interface is not allowed to lie

Every interaction must be backed by a real capability:

- "You will not have to repeat yourself" is true only because there is one Reality Model.
- "We will tell you before you have to ask" is true only because of Perceive → Understand → Judge → Communicate.
- "Someone owns this" is true only because of Orchestrate → Act → Verify.

If the operating model cannot make a promise true, the interface may not make it.

## People do not experience models. They experience organizations.

No one experiences a Reality Graph. They experience an organization that understands
reality exceptionally well. An interface's job is to let a person interact with that
organization, not to expose its machinery.

## What this means for the website

The website is one of the first places **reality enters the organization**. The
question is never "what does a visitor see." It is:

1. What reality is this person bringing in? (A daughter with "Dad is declining" and a
   physician with "I have a referral" bring different realities.)
2. Given that reality, what should their experience of it be?
3. What interaction delivers that experience — made true by a real capability?

Design from experience questions — what uncertainty exists now, what should disappear,
what should increase, what must never happen — then confirm the operating model can
keep every promise the design implies.
