# The Architecture of Experience — LOCKED

> Status: **LOCKED**, 2026-06-30. Frozen; changes only by deliberate amendment.
> Sits beneath `docs/VISION.md`. Supersedes the earlier two-axis draft.

## Canon (the locked wording)

> Reality is shared.
> Understanding is organizational.
> Decisions are accountable.
> Commitments are binding.
> Participant obligations determine what each person may know, must do, or should receive.
> Interactions deliver those obligations into the world.

## The architecture (the irreducible nouns)

```
Reality
   ↓
Record
   ↓
Understanding
   ↓
Decision
   ↓
Commitment
   ↓
Participant Obligation
   ↓
Interaction
```

The **Operating Cycle is the engine** that moves reality through the chain. It is not
a layer:

```
Perceive → Understand → Judge → Orchestrate → Act → Communicate → Verify → Learn
```

## What each noun uniquely owns

- **Reality** — what is actually true; external; one; unowned.
- **Record** — the persistent, canonical facts, including the organization's own
  decisions and commitments. (The Reality Graph.)
- **Understanding** — the regenerable synthesis of the Record ("what is going on").
  Owns no canonical state of its own. (The Reality Model.)
- **Decision** — what should happen. Normative, atomic, accountable, auditable; the
  locus of human sovereignty.
- **Commitment** — what the organization has bound itself to do: owned, scheduled,
  resourced; atomic. The unit that Verify checks and that agents execute against.
- **Participant Obligation** — for each participant, what they **may know, must do, or
  should receive**. Authority-bounded; channel-agnostic. The only place
  participant-specificity and Authority & Consent enter.
- **Interaction** — the consent-governed delivery of obligations into the world, and
  the inbound capture that re-enters as Reality.

(The concrete words/pixels/voice are *rendering* — sub-architecture, replaceable, not canon.)

## Invariants

- One shared reality; one organizational understanding; nothing forks.
- Every action traces to an accountable decision; nothing committed falls away unseen.
- Each participant receives exactly and only what they are owed and permitted —
  identically across every channel.
- An interaction may deliver only what its obligation contains, which derives only from
  what the operating model can actually do. **The interface cannot lie.**

## Self-reference

Decisions and Commitments, once made, are written back into the Record. The
organization perceives and verifies against its own commitments. The chain is a loop,
not a line.

## Universality

These nouns hold for any intelligent organization (test: would they exist if AlaraOS
ran Amazon, SpaceX, Kaiser, or the Department of Defense). Domain concepts (White Card,
EEOICPA, nurse) are instances that populate the nouns, never nouns themselves.

## Rejected alternatives

- **"Stakeholder Projection" / "Desired Experience" / "Stakeholder Experience of
  Reality"** — these name an *outcome* or a *rendering*, not a generative layer.
  Experience is the result of the architecture, not a layer in it.
- **"Standing"** (for Participant Obligation) — legally precise but less operational.
  Rejected in favor of **Participant Obligation**.
- **"Organizational Response Plan" as a primitive** — it is a *composite* (a set of
  Decisions and Commitments), not an irreducible noun.
- **Operating Cycle as a layer** — it is the engine (verbs), not a noun (state).

## Doctrine line

People do not experience records, models, or decisions. They experience the
organization. Their experience is the result of reality understood correctly, decided
well, and the obligation to them met honestly.

## What this means for the website

The website is one **Interaction** surface, and it is bidirectional: inbound it is a
*Perceive* surface (a visitor's reality enters the Record); outbound it delivers that
visitor's **Participant Obligation**. It is built by asking: what reality is this
person bringing, what are we obligated to give them, and which capability makes each
promise true. It is never built straight from architecture to pixels.
