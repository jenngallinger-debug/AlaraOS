# Competitive Kill List — UEW · Trusted Ally · CNS Cares

Surgical teardown of the three EEOICPA home-health competitors, and how Alara
takes their best, matches the table stakes, and exploits the gaps. Sourced from
live-site research (July 2026). Verbatim competitor lines in quotes.

---

## 0. The one-line verdict

All three lead with the **same** hook — *"up to $400,000 tax-free + no-cost home
care"* — and all three funnel to a **phone call**. Two of the three are
**private-equity-owned** hiding behind a founder story. The third
(Trusted Ally) is genuinely family-owned but **run by non-clinicians**.

**Nobody in this market is (a) owned by a working nurse today, (b) proactive with
technology, or (c) self-serve online.** That is the whole game. Alara is all three.

## 2. Per-competitor: their best, their gap

### UEW Healthcare (uewhealth.com) — the national brand
- **Best:** polished "whole-person care" brand; broad benefits stack ($400k, $2,500/impairment pt, $125k survivor, RECA $100k); ~25-state programmatic SEO footprint; clean Part B vs Part E education.
- **Gaps:** PE-owned since 2022; **templated** state pages (Nevada page is boilerplate with the state name swapped); **no patient testimonials on-site** (their only public reviews are mixed *employee* reviews citing "patient care no longer the top priority," 20+ patients/week); **no interactive tools**; duplicate/legacy URL sprawl (SEO debt); thin/absent White-Card "how to register" walkthrough; soft, generic CTAs.

### Trusted Ally (trustedally.com) — the money-forward challenger
- **Best:** sharpest money hook in the market — *"Your guide to tax-free compensation and free home healthcare through the EEOICPA White Card Program"* + *"up to $400,000."* Strong family-owned story (great-granddaughter of a nuclear worker), explicit anti-PE positioning, deep family-caregiver W2 benefits page, warm named-staff testimonials, a real blog.
- **Gaps:** **owners are not clinicians** (communications founder + social-entrepreneur CEO) — no RN at the top; HQ in **Colorado**, not local to Las Vegas; **no self-serve tools** (funnel is "call for a free eligibility review"); two competing domains (brand/SEO dilution); A+ but **not BBB-accredited**; money-first framing can read transactional.

### CNS Cares / Critical Nurse Staffing (cnscares.com) — the "nurse-founded" incumbent
- **Best:** strongest hero headline verbatim — *"You may be eligible for up to $400,000 in financial compensation and No Cost medical benefits"*; "founded by nurses" origin (2002); family-caregiver direct-employee model; **CareBeat blog + PDF newsletters + in-person educational lunch events** (incl. Las Vegas & Pahrump NTS workers); dedicated White-Card-denial content.
- **Gaps:** **PE-owned** (Revelstoke) with **CFO/CIO leadership** — the "founded by nurses" line is history, not present ownership; templated national state pages; mixed employee reviews ("management is the worst," "can't reach anyone," "no communication"); **no self-serve tools**; thin survivor-benefit content.

### A. Take their best — and do it better
1. **Their $400k money hero → ours, with the elevation they can't claim.** They stop at "we guide you to the money." Alara adds: *"and our own nurses deliver the care."* Guide + provider in one line. (Home hero now does this.)

### C. Ruthlessly exploit the gaps (what NONE of them have)
1. **NURSE-OWNED, TODAY — the master wedge.** Two are PE-owned; the third has non-clinician owners. Alara is the only agency where *the owner is the nurse who is accountable for your care.* Lead the "Why Alara" story with this, in Alara's own words.

## 5. The positioning fix (important)

**STOP:** The current home "Why Alara" moat uses *"the great-granddaughter of a
former nuclear weapons program worker"* and *"we answer to families, not
investors."* **Those are Trusted Ally's exact, owned positioning lines.** Leading
with them makes Alara look derivative and cedes the ground to the incumbent who
already owns them — the opposite of beating them.

**START:** Lead the moat with the wedge that is **uniquely Alara's and beats all
three at once**:

> **The only EEOICPA home-health agency in Las Vegas that is owned and run by the
> nurse accountable for your care — not private equity, not a call center.**
> Two of the biggest names in this space are owned by private-equity firms. The
> other is family-owned but run by marketers, not clinicians. At Alara, the owner
> is the nurse. And we don't just help you claim the benefit — we're the licensed
> agency that delivers the care.

Support with: nurse-owned + DON-led · AlaraOS stays ahead of your care · the
Navigator you can use yourself · genuinely Las Vegas-local · one named Care Guide.
State caregiver pay / independence as *quiet facts*, never the headline — and skip
the W-2/employee-model framing entirely (too on-the-nose, and it's the rivals' line).

(If the owner genuinely shares the nuclear-worker heritage, keep it as a quiet
authenticity note lower on the About page — never as the headline, because the
market already reads that line as Trusted Ally's.)
