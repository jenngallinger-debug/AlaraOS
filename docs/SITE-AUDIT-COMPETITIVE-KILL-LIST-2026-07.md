# Alara — Site Audit, Competitive Analysis, Gaps Profile & Kill List
**Date:** 2026-07 · **Scope:** full site (`public/*.html`) + the EEOICPA / White Card home-health competitive field.
**Frame:** the website should replace the first phone call — a family gets everything a caller would, and the machine funnels them to the one action that actually starts care: **a physician's order / referral**. The kill list is literal: how a nurse-owned, local, self-serve Alara beats each incumbent.

---

## The one fact the whole strategy hinges on

Home health under the White Card cannot start until a **treating physician signs a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN)**. The mechanics (DOL): the patient files **Form EE-17A**; DEEOIC sends **Form EE-17B** to the treating physician, who certifies they examined the patient within 60 days and specifies the hours/day and days/week of care. Only **MDs/DOs** may order it (NPs/PAs can't — an active reform fight). Sources: [DOL owcpmed EE-17A/EE-17B](https://owcpmed.dol.gov/portal/deeoic-initial-home-health-care-nursing-home-or-assisted-living-request-forms-ee-17a-and-ee-17b), [DOL HRHC brochure](https://www.dol.gov/sites/dolgov/files/owcp/energy/regs/compliance/brochure/HRHC.pdf), [Hickenlooper/Blackburn/Murray bill](https://www.hickenlooper.senate.gov/press_releases/hickenlooper-blackburn-murray-introduce-bipartisan-bill-to-expand-health-care-for-energy-workers/).

**Therefore the site's ultimate CTA is: get the physician to send the referral / order to Alara.** Everything else (Navigator, case review, guides) is the on-ramp to that. This is the audit's north star — and today the site under-builds it (see Gap G1).

# PART 1 — SITE AUDIT (surgical)

## 1A. User journeys — who lands, and where they should go
Four cohorts + referrers. Current routing:
| Cohort | Entry | Should end at |
|---|---|---|
| Has a White Card, needs care | white-card.html, home chooser | **Physician order → Alara** (start of care) |
| Might qualify (Test Site/DOE) | white-card fork, Navigator | **Case review → then physician LMN** |
| Federal/postal injured | owcp.html, Navigator | Physician order → Alara |
| Veteran | veterans.html | VA Community Care referral (honest: enrollment pending) |
| Physician / case manager | "Refer a Patient" nav | **Send referral to referrals@alarahomecare.com** |

**Finding:** the patient journeys are well built (Navigator → case review → guides). The **referrer journey and the physician-order handoff are the thinnest, most important part** and need to be the strongest (Gap G1).

## 1B. Broken paths — found & fixed this pass
- **7 dead deep-links** `qualify.html?node=ans-…` on programs.html / services.html. The Navigator never reads `?node=`, so every one dumped the user at Question 1 instead of the intended answer. **Fixed:** repointed to the real guide sections (`white-card.html#consequential`, `#medicare`, `#missed`, `owcp.html#covers`, `veterans.html#how`). ✅
- No missing files. (The audit script's other "broken" hits were false positives: query strings, `logo.svg`/`site.css`, JS-built href strings, and glossary anchors whose ids are generated by JS at runtime.)

## 1C. CTA coverage & the phone-push problem
- **Every page has a CTA.** ✅
- **Phone-as-a-button** (against the "don't push calls" rule) on services.html and las-vegas.html — literal `tel:` buttons in the hero. **Fixed:** demoted to self-serve ("See what we accept", "Start a case review"); phone stays in every header. ✅
- **Remaining softeners (P1, not yet done):** "Talk to a nurse" leads on nevada-test-site.html, other-doe-workers.html, reca.html read as phone-first. Recommend demoting to the standard quiet "or call" fallback.

# PART 3 — GAPS PROFILE

## The field's white space (what NOBODY does well)
1. **Self-serve tools.** No competitor has a real Benefit Navigator, program-comparison table, glossary, impairment estimator, or a self-serve case-review intake. They all gate everything behind a phone call, a town hall, or an outreach rep. **This is the entire opening.**
2. **Transparent, verifiable clinical ownership.** PE-owned (UEW, NCP, Giving), family-owned-not-clinical (Trusted Ally), or national-and-distant (PCM). CNS is the only nurse-founded rival, and it's regional with no tools.
3. **A clean, loud compliance/integrity posture** in a field with two multi-million-dollar fraud settlements and an active qui-tam suit.
4. **The physician-order path made effortless.** Everyone knows the LMN is the gate; nobody makes it easy or even explains it clearly to patients and doctors.
5. **Honest local trust signals** (reviews/BBB) rooted in Las Vegas.

## Alara's existing moats (keep pressing)
- **Nurse-owned + DON-reviews-every-start-of-care**, local to Las Vegas.
- **The self-serve stack no rival has:** Navigator, comparison table, glossary, impairment estimator, **case-review tool**, deep sourced guides — the site *is* the town hall, on demand.
- **Clean record + honesty** (e.g., the Veterans page states enrollment is pending rather than faking it).

## Alara's own gaps (to close)
- **G1 (P0): the physician-referral / order path is thin.** No patient-facing "here's the one form your doctor signs (EE-17A → EE-17B/LMN)" explainer, and the referrer flow is a callback form, not a structured referral submission to referrals@alarahomecare.com.
- **G2 (P1): family-caregiver ("get a family member paid") depth** is a one-liner; competitors (Trusted Ally, Atomic) market a full "Family Health Aide" program. Build the how-to.
- **G3 (P1): RECA prominence.** Trusted Ally markets the 2025 RECA expansion loudly; Alara has a good RECA page but should surface the Dec 31, 2027 deadline more.
- **G4 (P2): trust signals** — no reviews/testimonials/BBB on-site (owner-blocked until real quotes exist; worth prioritizing off-site).
- **G5 (P2): events presence** — the whole field wins offline via Cold War Patriots town halls and the LV Day of Remembrance. Alara is web-only; a modest local-events presence would complete the pincer.

# PART 4 — KILL LIST (how Alara kills each)

**Overarching kill — the one sentence:** *"The town hall, the sales call, and the benefits rep — all on your phone, at 2 a.m., from the only home-health agency here that's owned by the nurse accountable for your care."* No competitor can copy the self-serve stack quickly, and none can claim local nurse-ownership.

1. **Kill UEW** — with **integrity**. UEW paid **$9M to the DOJ** for billing for care never provided and telling caregivers to overstate hours. Alara's counter: a visible, plain **"how our billing works / what we never do"** integrity statement, DON-reviewed starts, and the transparent nurse-owner. Never name UEW; own the value ("your care is billed for exactly what happened, reviewed by a nurse").
2. **Kill Nuclear Care Partners** — with **local + calm**. NCP is PE-owned, fights an active federal FCA suit, and **sues its own ex-employees and Nevada rivals**. Alara's counter: born-in-Las-Vegas, nurse-owned, not litigating the neighborhood — the calm local expert vs. the hostile out-of-state incumbent.
3. **Kill PCM / Cold War Patriots** — with **self-serve that beats the town hall**. CWP's edge is the in-person event funnel. Alara's Navigator + comparison table + case-review tool deliver the same "what am I owed / how do I start" answer instantly, without waiting for a seminar. Add a light local-events presence (G5) to contest the offline turf.
4. **Kill Trusted Ally** — with **actual clinical ownership + tools**. Their whole pitch is "family-owned, not investors" and "W-2 nurses" — but the **owners are an entrepreneur + a family heir, not nurses**, and they're **HQ'd in Colorado** with **~1 Las Vegas review and no BBB accreditation**, and **no estimator/glossary**. Alara out-claims them: *nurse-owned* (not just family-owned), *actually in Las Vegas*, with the self-serve tools they lack.
5. **Kill CNS Cares** — with **local depth + the tool stack**. CNS shares the nurse-founded angle but is regional (NV/NM/TN) and tool-less. Alara wins on Las Vegas / Nevada Test Site specificity and the self-serve experience.
6. **Neutralize the small locals (Atomic Legacy, AWA, NWA)** — with **credibility and depth**: sourced guides, a real estimator, DON-led clinical governance, and the case-review tool make Alara the obvious serious choice.

## Addendum — three more pieces of white space (high value)
- **"Ungated & private" is a real weapon.** Every competitor's eligibility quiz is lead-capture-gated (phone + SMS consent → sales callback). Alara's Navigator and quick-check are **ungated and save nothing** — say that out loud ("no forms, nothing saved, no sales call"). It's both a trust signal and a genuine product difference.
- **Own the home-health-HOURS black box (new G6, P1).** How care hours are set, the ~6-month re-authorization cycle, and — the single most-searched, least-answered question — **how to get your hours increased** ("contact your Claims Examiner" is all anyone offers). No competitor explains this. A clear explainer would own the highest-anxiety topic in the category. Source: [DOL HRHC manual Ch. 3-1000](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/owcp/energy/regs/compliance/PolicyandProcedures/proceduremanualhtml/unifiedpm/Unifiedpm_part3/Chapter3-1000HRHC).
- **"Local, nurse-led, no traveling nurses" is the sharpest under-owned line.** Only small regionals (e.g., Atomic Legacy: *"don't play phone tag with an out-of-state company… no traveling nurses"*) voice it, and the nationals' own employee reviews (20+ patients/week, contractor pay problems) give it teeth. Systematize it as Alara's category position.
