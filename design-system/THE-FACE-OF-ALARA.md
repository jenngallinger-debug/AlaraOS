# The Face of Alara

> Creative direction for the homepage. **This is a derivation, not an invention.**
> Everything here traces to three closed sources: the **Constitution**
> (`docs/CONSTITUTION.md`), the **Eight Moments** (the lived experiences that produce
> Alara's reputation), and the reputation itself — **"They had us."** If a visual
> decision cannot be traced to one of those, it does not belong on the page, and it
> is not here.
>
> The brand is fixed: the five-color neutral palette, Jost + Inter, the arch, warmth
> from photography only. See `design-system/BRAND.md` (the design source of truth).
> Nothing below reinterprets the brand; it expresses it.

---

## What this page is

The homepage is **the first member of the care team.** It begins carrying burden
before a human ever speaks. So it is not designed as a website. It is designed as a
**threshold** — the moment a person who has been holding everything arrives somewhere
that can hold it for them.

The supplied hero photograph is that idea made literal: a wide stone staircase rising
to a single arched door, warm brass at the top of a long pale ascent. Not a lobby, not
a clinic, not a funnel. A **place with a door, and someone behind it.** Every decision
on this page serves the feeling of arriving there.

The standard is one sentence: in three seconds a person should think *"these people
understand my world"* — not *"this is home healthcare,"* not *"this is a nice website,"*
not *"this is an AI."* **Recognition first. Conversation earned.**

### The Eight Moments (the source we derive from)

Alara's reputation — *"They had us"* — is produced by eight lived moments. These are
the experiences people remember, and they are the spine of this page. Each one is also
a clause of the Constitution, made visible:

| # | The moment people remember | Constitutional commitment it expresses |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Nothing falls through.** | Accountability — *"nothing entrusted to it is allowed to fall away unseen."* |
| 2 | **They never have to chase anyone.** | Accountability — *"What it takes responsibility for, it carries to completion."* |
| 3 | **Someone always remembers.** | Human Primacy — the person is held in mind, not in a queue. |
| 4 | **The truth is told gently.** | Integrity — *"faces contradictions rather than hiding them,"* told with care. |
| 5 | **Burdens disappear.** | Human Primacy — *"Alara exists to be the one who holds it."* |
| 6 | **Promises are kept.** | Accountability — *"bound by what it owes… to its promises."* |
| 7 | **Someone is always one step ahead.** | Human Primacy + Accountability — held, anticipated, never abandoned to coordinate alone. |
| 8 | **"They had us."** | All five commitments at once — the sum a person feels when the other seven are true. |

Authority & Consent and Human Sovereignty (commitments 3 and 4 of the Constitution)
are expressed not by a section but by **restraint**: the page never pressures, never
manufactures urgency, never takes a decision out of the visitor's hands, and never asks
for more than a person is ready to give. The absence of those tactics *is* the
expression.

## Deliverable 1 — Spatial Language

*Before any UI. What does Alara feel like, physically? How does it breathe? Where does
the eye travel? What creates calm, trust, recognition?*

**The material.** Alara is built of warm stone and quiet air. Travertine greige,
paper, the soft grey of mist — the five neutral swatches and nothing else. The only
warmth is the brass of the door in the photograph; the interface itself never reaches
for color to do an emotion's job. This is deliberate: **a calm room does not shout.**
The richness is in the light and the space, not the paint.

**How it breathes.** Slowly. The page is mostly negative space, and the space is the
point — it is the visual equivalent of someone unhurried, someone who has time for you.
Section rhythm is generous (`clamp(72px, 9vw, 144px)`), measures are short, lines are
long-leaded (1.7). Nothing is crowded because **a crowded page tells an overwhelmed
person they are still alone with too much.** Air is the first burden removed.

**Where the eye travels.** Down, then up. The hero composition mirrors the photograph:
the eye descends the staircase and rises to the door. Down the page, the journey is a
single calm vertical — one idea per screen, never a grid to scan, never columns to
compare. There is always exactly one place to look and, at the bottom of each screen,
exactly one quiet thing you may do. **The eye is never made to work**, because a person
in crisis has no attention to spare.

**What creates calm.** Stillness and symmetry. Hairlines, never shadows — nothing
floats, nothing hovers, nothing demands. Type sits on a strict baseline; edges align;
motion, where it exists, is a single slow fade (220ms) and never a bounce. Calm is
manufactured by the *absence* of the things that raise cortisol: no pop-ups, no
countdowns, no chat bubble lunging from the corner, no carousel moving on its own.

**What creates trust.** Permanence and exactness. The arch is the signature of
permanence — it is in the logo's doorway, the hero, the founder's frame, the closing
section. It says *this organization was built to last and was built for you.* Exactness
— precise spacing, sourced claims kept honest and low, a real named human — says *the
people here are careful, and careful people don't let things fall through.* Trust is not
claimed in a tagline; it is **demonstrated by how the page behaves**, exactly as the
Constitution says Alara earns trust ("not by claiming to be trustworthy, but by how it
consistently behaves").

**What creates recognition.** The words, not the pictures. Recognition is the feeling
*"they understand people like me,"* and it comes from naming the visitor's real
emotional situation — the holding, the tangle, the fear that something will be missed —
in plain, warm, exact language, before a single service, program, or credential is
mentioned. The photograph supports it (a threshold, an arrival); the language carries it.

## Deliverable 2 — Experience Blueprint

*The emotional journey through the homepage. Where recognition, relief, curiosity, and
trust happen, and where conversation is finally earned.*

The page is one descent, emotionally tuned. Each stop does exactly one emotional job and
hands the visitor to the next:

| Stop | Emotional job | What happens inside the visitor |
|---|---|---|
| **1. The Threshold** (hero) | **Recognition + Relief + Permission** | *"I am in the right place. They understand what I'm carrying. I'm allowed to set it down."* |
| **2. The Tangle** | **Relief deepens** | *"They already know what my world looks like, the whole impossible knot of it, and they're saying they'll hold it."* |
| **3. What They Remember** (the Eight Moments) | **Recognition becomes belief** | *"This is what it would actually feel like to be cared for by them. This is what 'they had us' means."* |
| **4. The Person Behind the Door** | **Trust begins** | *"A real, named, accountable human stands behind this. I know who is responsible."* |
| **5. And It's Real** (legitimacy as reward) | **Trust is confirmed** | *"It's also legitimate, covered, and most likely free. The feeling and the facts agree."* |
| **6. The Invitation** | **Conversation is earned** | *"I can tell them what's happening now. It costs me almost nothing to begin."* |

**Why this order.** The brief is explicit: recognition first, conversation earned.
A person arriving here is not shopping for a vendor; they are frightened and overloaded.
If the page leads with programs, credentials, or a form, it asks them to do work and to
trust strangers before they feel understood — and they leave. So the page **earns the
right to ask** in stages: first it shows it understands (1–2), then it shows what being
held feels like (3), then it shows who is accountable (4), only then does it surface the
proof and the practical truth a skeptic needs (5), and only at the very end does it ask
for anything (6). By the time the ask comes, it is a relief to answer it.

**Where the practical truth lives.** The hero now states the positioning plainly —
*home health that knows your benefits* — and names the programs and "most patients pay
nothing" in the subhead, per owner direction that this is home health and education, not
therapy. The *depth* (per-program coverage, DON review, full credentials) is still the
reward for scrolling (stop 5), where it confirms the hero's claim instead of crowding it.
The earlier draft kept all benefits language out of the hero; that read as too soft, so
the rule changed from "no education in the hero" to "lead with competence, keep the long
proof below."

## Deliverable 4 — High-Fidelity Design

Built as a self-contained, viewable page at **`public/home.html`** (open it directly in
a browser). It uses the real assets — `public/logo.svg` and `public/hero-arches.webp` —
and only the canonical brand. Below, every consequential decision and the burden it
removes.

**Palette — the five neutral swatches, nothing else.** espresso `#342E2E`, taupe
`#817A7A`, greige `#D9D8D3`, mist `#E8E8E8`, paper `#FBFBFB`. *No gold in the interface*
(per BRAND.md's reconciliation log, which is the final word over `tokens.css`'s lingering
gold). No blue. No clinical brightness. The only warmth on the page is the brass door
inside the photograph. *Which burden does this remove?* The visual noise that would tell
an overwhelmed person they are in one more loud, selling place. A quiet room lowers
cortisol; that is the whole job of the color decision.

**Type — Jost Light (wordmark, eyebrows) + Inter (headings, body). No serif.** Body 17px
minimum, line-height 1.7, links underlined, focus-visible rings, everything legible at
375px and to a senior reader. *Burden removed:* the effort of reading. Authority comes
from the typographic restraint and the space around the words, not from a display serif
the brand doesn't use.

**The hero does its three jobs in a competent register.** Recognition, relief, and
permission — but stated as a capable home-health operator, not a grief counselor (owner
direction: *"this isn't therapy, it's home health and education"*). Recognition now comes
from naming the visitor's exact situation plainly — *home health that knows your
benefits* — and relief from the competence claim — *and handles the rest.* The
subhead names what it is (skilled nursing), who it's for (White Card, veteran, Medicare),
and the hook (*most patients pay nothing*). *Burden removed:* the work of coordinating
care and decoding benefits, plus the skeptic's "is this real." The discipline is no longer
"say nothing concrete" — it is "say only what's true and load-bearing, in plain words."

**One action per screen, and the action is small.** The primary CTA everywhere is *"Tell
us your number"* with the phone as the calm alternative and *"we answer within the hour"*
as reassurance. Phone-first because this audience calls, and because asking for a phone
number is the smallest possible beginning. *Burden removed:* the burden of starting. No
forms to figure out, no account, no program to name.

**The Eight Moments are a sequence, not a grid.** Plain hairline-separated statements in
the family's voice, ending in the large pull-quote *"They had us."* No icons, no cards,
no columns. *Burden removed:* the cognitive load of a feature grid; and the emotional
falseness of decorating grief with iconography. The reputation is stated as memory, the
way it is actually earned.

**The founder is rendered through words and an arch frame, not a stock photo.** Fabricating
a portrait would violate Integrity and the brand's ban on imagery inconsistent with Alara.
The real photograph drops into the arch frame when it exists. *Burden removed:* the
suspicion that comes from generic "caregiver hugging patient" stock — its absence is a
trust signal.

**Legitimacy is low and quiet, and it is the reward for scrolling.** A single paragraph
and one hairline rule of typographic marks — never shiny badges. *Burden removed:* the
skeptic's and the professional's burden ("is this real and covered?"), answered *after*
the page has earned the right to be believed.

**Motion is one slow fade, and it respects `prefers-reduced-motion`.** No carousels, no
parallax, no auto-play, no floating chat. *Burden removed:* the low-grade anxiety of a
page that moves on its own. Stillness is care.

**The arch recurs as the signature of permanence** — logo mark, hero photograph, founder
frame, the arched top of the closing invitation. *Burden removed:* the feeling of a
temporary, fly-by-night agency. Repetition of the arch says *built to last, and built for
you.*

## The one reconciliation, stated plainly

`BRAND.md` records that Notion holds "approved homepage copy" whose hero leads with the
federal-benefits headline. The governing creative direction for *this* work states the
hero may not educate or explain programs. These conflict only about **placement**, not
truth. Resolution: the federal-benefits truth is kept verbatim-faithful but **moved from
the hero to stop 5** ("And it's real"), where the HOMEPAGE-STRATEGY itself says programs
and credentials belong ("NOT above the fold … the programs … the reward for scrolling").
Nothing was dropped; one thing was moved down one screen so recognition could come first.
Every other element traces, without conflict, to the Constitution, the Eight Moments, and
"They had us."
