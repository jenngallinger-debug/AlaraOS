Programs and benefits we accept

The federal benefit programs we specialize in.

When a physician orders skilled home health for a covered condition, these federal programs pay for it in full, at no cost to you. Below is who each program is for and what it covers at home. We confirm what applies to you and handle the authorizations.

We work with
White Card (EEOICPA) RECA OWCP / FECA VA Community Care TRICARE West, ask us

EEOICPA / White Card

For Nevada Test Site, DOE, and atomic weapons workers.

For former Nevada Test Site and Nevada National Security Site workers, Department of Energy and Atomic Weapons Employer workers, and eligible survivors with an accepted EEOICPA condition.

Energy workers DOE contractors Survivors

What it covers at home

  • Skilled nursing
  • Wound care
  • Physical and occupational therapy
  • Home health aide services
  • Caregiver training

And more we help you reach: travel reimbursement, equipment, and the benefits most people miss.

Cost

Generally no out-of-pocket cost for covered care tied to the accepted condition. No premiums, copays, or deductibles for those covered services.

Worth mentioning, and often missed

Consequential conditions can be added to the card. A family member already providing daily care may be paid as a home health aide with registered-nurse supervision. The White Card and Medicare can be used together.

Source: U.S. Department of Labor, EEOICP · dol.gov/agencies/owcp/energy

OWCP / FECA

For federal and postal workers with an accepted injury or illness.

For federal and postal (USPS) workers with an OWCP-accepted work-related injury or illness.

Federal workers Postal workers

What it covers at home

  • Skilled nursing
  • Wound care
  • Physical and occupational therapy
  • Home health aide services

Cost

Generally no out-of-pocket cost when the claim is accepted and care is authorized. Coverage requires a physician order and OWCP authorization.

The complete OWCP / FECA guide → See what applies to you →

Source: U.S. Department of Labor, OWCP/FECA · dol.gov/agencies/owcp/FECA

VA Community Care / TriWest

For veterans authorized for care in the community.

For enrolled veterans authorized for community care when the VA cannot readily provide it directly. In Region 4, which includes Nevada, the network is administered by TriWest.

Veterans Community care

What it covers at home

  • Skilled nursing
  • Wound care
  • Physical and occupational therapy
  • Home health aide services

Cost

No cost to veterans authorized for community care. The VA authorizes and pays directly. A VA referral, authorization, and provider order are required.

Network participation and any PACT Act details can change. Ask us and we will confirm what applies to you before you rely on it.

Community Care in the Library → See what applies to you →

Source: U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, Community Care · va.gov/communitycare

TRICARE West

For eligible military families and retirees.

For eligible active-duty family members, retirees, and dependents in Southern Nevada. TriWest administers TRICARE West in Nevada.

Military families Retirees

What it covers at home

  • Skilled home health services for eligible plans

Cost

Coverage depends on the specific TRICARE plan. Patients should confirm their plan coverage and eligibility.

TRICARE West participation and the services that qualify vary by plan. Ask us and we will confirm what applies before you rely on it.
