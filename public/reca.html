Benefits/RECA
Radiation Exposure Compensation Act

RECA is open again. The deadline to file is December 31, 2027.

RECA compensates people made sick by the government’s nuclear-weapons testing and uranium work. It lapsed in 2024, and in 2025 it was reauthorized and expanded. If you were downwind of the testing, present at the Nevada Test Site, or a uranium worker, you may qualify, and a RECA award can open the door to the White Card that covers home health at no cost to you.

File by December 31, 2027
01What RECA is

RECA is a one-time payment from the U.S. Department of Justice for people who developed certain illnesses after radiation exposure from the nation’s atomic-weapons program. It is compassionate and non-adversarial: you do not have to prove fault, only that you meet the eligibility criteria. It is separate from the White Card, which is run by the Department of Labor.

After expiring in 2024, RECA was reauthorized and expanded in 2025, and it is accepting claims again through the Department of Justice. The window is not permanent, which is why the December 31, 2027 deadline matters.

02Who may qualify

RECA covers three groups. The Nevada Test Site connection makes the first two especially relevant here.

Downwinders

People who lived in a covered area during the years of atmospheric testing and later developed a covered cancer. The 2025 law expanded the covered areas; which ones apply to you is determined by the Department of Justice.

Onsite participants

People who were physically present at a U.S. atmospheric nuclear test, including the Nevada Test Site, and later developed a covered illness.

Uranium workers

Miners, millers, ore transporters, and related workers in the uranium industry during the covered years. The 2025 law extended the covered work period.

03What it pays
A one-time lump sum

Under the 2025 reauthorization, the payment for these categories is reported at $100,000. If the worker has passed away, eligible survivors may claim.

Amounts, covered areas, and eligibility are set by the Department of Justice and can change. Confirm the current figure and whether you qualify at justice.gov/civil/reca. This page is general information, not a determination.

04Why this matters for your care

RECA pays cash. It does not pay for care. The White Card does.

Here is the part most people miss. If you are a uranium worker who receives RECA, you may also qualify under EEOICPA for the White Card, which covers medical care, including skilled home health, at no cost to you, plus an additional payment. RECA is the cash; the White Card is the care. That is where Alara comes in.
