Benefits/RECA
Radiation Exposure Compensation Act

RECA is open again.

It compensates people made sick by the government’s nuclear testing and uranium work. It lapsed, and in 2025 it was reopened and expanded.

The deadline to file
December 31, 2027

The window is not permanent. If it may apply to you, this is the time.

$100,000

A one-time payment for those who qualify, under the 2025 reauthorization. Survivors may claim if the worker has passed. Amounts and eligibility are set by the Department of Justice and can change. Confirm the current figure at justice.gov/civil/reca.

Who may qualify

RECA covers three groups. The Nevada Test Site connection makes the first two especially close to home.

01
Downwinders

You lived in a covered area during the years of atmospheric testing and later developed a covered cancer. The 2025 law expanded the covered areas.

DOJ determines the area
02
Onsite participants

You were physically present at a U.S. atmospheric nuclear test, including the Nevada Test Site, and later developed a covered illness.

Nevada Test Site
03
Uranium workers

You were a miner, miller, ore transporter, or related worker in the uranium industry during the covered years. The 2025 law extended that window.

Opens the White Card
The part most people miss

RECA pays cash. It does not pay for care. The White Card does.

If you are a uranium worker who receives RECA, you may also qualify for the White Card, which covers medical care, including skilled home health, at no cost to you, plus an additional payment. RECA is the cash. The White Card is the care. That is where Alara comes in.
