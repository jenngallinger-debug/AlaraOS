Learning Hub

Federal benefits, explained.

Most families never claim the full scope of what they have earned. These are the terms that matter for home health, reviewed by a nurse and linked to the federal source. They are grouped by program, so you can read the family of benefits you came for.

Compare the programs

Which program is yours, side by side.

Five programs pay for home health, each in a different situation. Find the row that sounds like you, then read that guide.

Program Who it’s for What triggers it Cash compensation What it covers at home Your cost Deadline
White Card (EEOICPA) Nevada Test Site, DOE, or atomic-weapons workers — in any job — and their survivors Worked at a covered site and later became ill Up to $400,000, tax-free (lump sums plus impairment) Nursing, therapy, home health aide, social work, caregiver training, equipment, home changes, travel No cost to you None — you can file or reopen anytime
OWCP / FECA Federal and postal employees with a work injury or illness OWCP accepts the claim and a physician orders care Wage-loss and medical benefits (set by your claim) Nursing, physical and occupational therapy, home health aide, medical social work No cost to you File promptly after the injury or diagnosis
VA Community Care Veterans enrolled with the VA who need care at home The VA authorizes home health through Community Care — (this is care, not cash) Nursing, physical and occupational therapy, home health aide, medical social work No cost to you None
RECA Downwinders, onsite test participants, and uranium workers — and survivors A covered illness tied to radiation or uranium work A one-time $100,000 payment — (RECA is cash; uranium workers can open the White Card for care) — December 31, 2027 (hard deadline)
Medicare People 65+ or with a qualifying disability Homebound, with a physician order for skilled care — (this is care, not cash) Intermittent skilled nursing, therapy, limited home health aide, medical social work No cost to you for covered home health None
